	The process of validating all the input to an application before using it.

Adware:
	Any software package which automatically renders advertisements.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adware

Browser hijacking:
	The modification of a web browser's settings by malware.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Browser_hijacking

Payload:
	The harmful results of malicious software such as worms, viruses and Trojans.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Payload_(computing)

Race condition:
	A type of flaw in an software system where the output is dependent on the sequence of timing of other uncontrollable events.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Race_condition

Spam:
	The use of electronic messaging systems to send unsolicited bulk messages, especially advertising, indiscriminately.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spam_(electronic)

Checksum:
	A fixed-size datum computed from an arbitrary block of digital data for the purpose of detecting accidental errors that may have been introduced during its transmission or storage.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Checksum

Variant:
	A different form of the same object.	
	http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/variant

Privilege confusion:
	The process of fooling other computer programs to misuse its authority.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confused_deputy_problem

Spyware:
	A type of malware installed on computers that collects information about users without their knowledge.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spyware

Sniffer:
	A software or a piece of hardware that can intercept and log traffic passing over a whole of part of a digital network.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Packet_annalyzer

Firewall:
	A software or hardware used to help keep a network secure by analyzing network traffic and determined whether it should be allowed or not.
	http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firewall_(computing)
